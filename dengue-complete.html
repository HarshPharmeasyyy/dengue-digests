Prevention & Protection

Simple steps to protect yourself and your family from dengue

Eliminate Standing Water

Remove stagnant water from flower pots, containers, and coolers. Check and clean water storage regularly.

Use Mosquito Protection

Apply mosquito repellent, use nets while sleeping, and wear long-sleeved clothes during peak hours.

Maintain Clean Environment

Keep surroundings clean, ensure proper drainage, and trim overgrown vegetation around your home.

Monitor Symptoms

Watch for fever, headache, body aches, and rash. Seek immediate medical attention if symptoms appear.

Peak Hour Awareness

Aedes mosquitoes are most active during dawn and dusk. Take extra precautions during these times.

Community Action

Participate in community clean-up drives and report potential breeding sites to local authorities.

Urgent: Seek Immediate Medical Attention

If you experience sudden onset of high fever, severe headache, eye pain, muscle aches, or skin rash, consult a healthcare provider immediately. Early diagnosis and proper medical care can prevent complications.

Recommended Tests

For suspected dengue cases, NS1 antigen (days 1-7), IgM antibodies (days 4-10), and platelet count monitoring are essential. Consult your doctor for appropriate testing based on symptom onset.

Key Takeaways

Essential insights for dengue prevention and management

Prevention is Key

With no specific treatment for dengue, prevention remains our strongest defense. Simple measures like eliminating standing water and using mosquito protection can reduce transmission risk by up to 70%.

Early Detection Saves Lives

Early diagnosis and proper medical care can reduce complications by 80%. Monitor symptoms closely, especially during peak season (July-November), and seek immediate medical attention when needed.

Stay Protected, Stay Informed

Knowledge and prevention are your best tools against dengue. Share this information with your family and community to help protect everyone.
